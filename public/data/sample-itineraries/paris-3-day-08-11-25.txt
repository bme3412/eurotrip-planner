Paris in 3 Days — Best‑of Itinerary (Improved Report)

Audience: First‑time visitors seeking icons + neighborhood charm with minimal backtracking.
Best months: April–June, September–October (shoulder = pleasant temps, manageable crowds).
Pace: ~8–9 hrs out + breaks; add removals if traveling with kids.

Pre‑book checklist (do this now)
- Louvre timed entry (choose late morning or evening hours)
- Sainte‑Chapelle time slot
- Eiffel Tower timed summit OR pick Montparnasse Tower for Eiffel skyline views
- Seine cruise sunset slot (optional)
- Museum Pass: worth it if entering 3+ paid sights across Days 1–2

What you’ll see
- Day 1: Historic Core (Île de la Cité), Louvre, Tuileries, optional Seine cruise
- Day 2: Left Bank art (Orsay, Rodin), Rue Cler, Eiffel Tower area, Invalides
- Day 3: Montmartre village + Sacré‑Cœur, Arc de Triomphe rooftop, Champs‑Élysées, Palais Garnier

Navigation
- Metro/contactless: tap‑in works (per‑ride ~€2.15). Buy Navigo Easy if preferred.
- Walking: Paris is compact; this route keeps transfers short and scenic.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 1 — Historic Core, Louvre, Seine
Morning (09:00–11:30)
- Île de la Cité warm‑up: Pont Neuf ➝ Notre‑Dame exterior (restoration views)
- Sainte‑Chapelle (45–60 min, timed). Tip: arrive 10–15 min before slot.

Lunch (11:45–12:45)
- Latin Quarter bistro or crêperie near Place Saint‑Michel.

Afternoon (13:15–16:15)
- Louvre highlights (2–3 hrs). Enter via Carrousel du Louvre underground.
  Focus: Winged Victory, Mona Lisa, Raft of the Medusa, Venus de Milo, plus 2 rooms you love.
- Tuileries Garden coffee by the basins.

Sunset/Evening (18:30–21:00)
- Optional Seine cruise (golden hour) boarding near Pont Neuf or Eiffel area.
- Dinner: Saint‑Germain (classic brasserie) or Le Marais (modern bistronomy).

Pro tips
- Louvre late openings are calmer. Curate ~10–12 works; don’t attempt the full museum.
- Sainte‑Chapelle best when sunlit; morning slots often glow.

Rain swaps
- Add Musée de l’Orangerie (Water Lilies, 60–75 min) in place of garden time.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 2 — Left Bank Art, Rue Cler, Eiffel Area
Morning (09:30–12:00)
- Musée d’Orsay (2 hrs). Head directly to Impressionists (5th floor) then circle back.
- Walk or short Metro to Musée Rodin (garden + The Thinker, ~60 min).

Lunch (12:15–13:15)
- Rue Cler market street picnic (boulangerie + fromagerie) or simple café lunch.

Afternoon (13:45–16:45)
- Eiffel Tower area: Trocadéro viewpoint ➝ timed summit/2nd floor (if booked).
- Les Invalides (Napoleon’s tomb) optional 45 min.

Evening (19:00–22:00)
- Saint‑Germain dinner. Optional jazz set (consider Caveau de la Huchette/Le Duc des Lombards).

Pro tips
- If Eiffel summit sells out, book Montparnasse Tower for skyline views of the Eiffel Tower at night.
- Rodin garden shines in spring/fall; in summer heat, go earlier.

Rain swaps
- Replace Rue Cler picnic with covered passages (e.g., Passage des Panoramas) + café stop.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 3 — Montmartre, Arc de Triomphe, Champs‑Élysées, Opera
Morning (09:00–11:30)
- Montmartre village loop: Abbesses ➝ Rue de l’Abreuvoir ➝ Place du Tertre.
- Sacré‑Cœur (free). Dome climb if weather is clear.

Lunch (11:45–12:45)
- Around Abbesses (crêpes/cafés) or take Metro toward Étoile for quick brasserie.

Afternoon (13:15–16:15)
- Arc de Triomphe rooftop (45 min). Golden‑hour potential if delayed.
- Champs‑Élysées stroll toward Place de la Concorde; detour to Galeries Lafayette rooftop (free terrace views).
- Palais Garnier (Opera) interior self‑guided (45 min) if time/interest.

Evening (19:00–21:30)
- Le Marais dinner (falafel lane, bistronomy, natural‑wine bars) or Canal Saint‑Martin for a relaxed finale.

Pro tips
- Best viewpoints: Sacré‑Cœur steps at sunrise; Arc de Triomphe near sunset.
- For a quieter Montmartre, start earlier and avoid Place du Tertre peak hours.

Rain swaps
- Centre Pompidou (modern art) or covered passages near Grands Boulevards.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
Food short‑list (reserve where possible)
- Classics: Bistrot Paul Bert (steak frites), Josephine Chez Dumonet (traditional), Bouillon Pigalle (budget).
- Crêpes: Breizh Café (multiple locations).
- Bakery & sweets: Du Pain et des Idées (morning), Pierre Hermé (macarons).
- Markets: Marché des Enfants Rouges (Marais), Rue Cler (Day 2).

Budget snapshot (per person, indicative)
- Sights: €35–€70/day (passes/timed entries vary)
- Food: €25–€60/day (lunch + dinner, moderate; wine/dessert add more)
- Transport: ~€6–€10/day (2–4 Metro rides; walking covers much)

Safety & etiquette
- Watch for pickpockets in crowd zones (Trocadéro, Metro, Louvre). Zip bags.
- Basic French greetings help; tipping is modest (round up or ~5–10% for great service).

Logistics & pacing notes
- Start early to front‑load icons, then slow down after lunch.
- Slot heavy indoor sights midday (cooling/AC) and scenic outdoor time near sunset.

Optional extensions (extra time)
- Versailles day trip (palace + gardens; pre‑book) or Giverny (seasonal).
- Le Marais boutique walk + Place des Vosges; Canal Saint‑Martin aperitif.

Outcome
In three days you’ll cover the icons without burning out, balance marquee museums with charming streets, and end each day in neighborhoods perfect for dinner and night views.
